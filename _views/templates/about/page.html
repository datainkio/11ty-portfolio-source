Education

My education is in social anthropology. As a student I explored how the relationship between language and tool use shapes human behavior and social structure. By analyzing how we describe and change the world around us, I uncovered how these elements influence societal norms and relationships.

Those years spent studying the interrelationships between scientific models of the human being continue to shape my values and methodology as a designer by emphasizing a holistic understanding of human behavior.

Today, as a visual creative, I'm often hands on encouraging teams to play, remix, and look at things sidewaysâ€”using customer and business insights, brand, storytelling, and human centered design to create differentiated products. I spend as much time sweating UI pixels and UX flows as I do sitting in workshops and boardrooms, discussing client's user needs, technical constraints, product strategy, and business objectives.

Collaboration

I apply this expertise across a wide range of domains, delivering solutions for global brands, museums, artists, musicians, hospitals, schools, and even the federal government. The audiences and business goals change, but the priority of maintaining focus on human-centered design remains constant.

It is work with a real impact. As the design manager for federal projects with Bixal, my teams and I increased equitable access to federal benefits and services for 330 million Americans, connected small businesses with $4.2 billion in funding, helped reduce the number of annual cases of foodborne illness by 7 million, and helped establish the United States Web Design System. As a developer and product designer with IBM Interactive, I led the design for the company's first application of Watson AI to healthcare, helped design and build both the world's first global platform for democratizing access to pediatric expertise, and helped design and build the first platform uniting urban data from cities across the globe.

Contributions
