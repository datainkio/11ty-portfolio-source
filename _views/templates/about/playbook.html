In my twenty-odd years of working as a user experience designer I have worked with a range of industries. Success is defined differently by each, and companies and organizations find their own paths to get there.

All succesful projects pay attention to the same seven practices:

  1. Shared understanding of goals and audiences
  2. A shared north star
  3. Measurement
  4. Cadence
  5. Tangibility
  6. Analysis
  7. Reflection

  1. Maintain a shared understanindg of business goals and target audiences.

    The team's understanding of business goals and target audiences.

  2. Maintain alignment towards a shared north star.

    Alignment is about keeping the team focused on the same north star.

  3. Identify what you are measuring and the questions you want to answer.

    What are you looking at and what do you want to know about it?

  4. Find a cadence that integrates the plays.

    Cadence is more than the rhythm of work. It's also workflow.

  5. Prioritize tangibility.

    Take the actionable insights produced by analysis and turn them into something tangible.

  6. Evaluate research and testing outcomes.

    Analysis is about looking at what you've measured and identifying the actionable insights within.

  7. Reflect on where you've been.

    Continuously refine both the product and the team culture.
